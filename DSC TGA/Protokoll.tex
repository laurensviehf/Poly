\section{Questions}

        \subsection{Question 1}

            The "Big" number indicates how many monomers are needed to make one full 360° rotation.
            The small number in the index says how many 360° rotations are needed until the order gets repeated.\\
            For example in the case of $8_5$ Polybutene. It needs 8 monomers to complete one 360° rotation and 5 full rotation until the order starts from the start.

        \subsection{Question 2}

            i-PP can be made by using the Ziegler-Natta-catalysis. 
            In this case catalysts like \ch{TiCl3} on \ch{MgCl2}-carrier are used in combination with aluminumalkenes.
            It can also be done via Metallocene-catalysis, this technique is specially used to control the tacticity and results in high graded i-PP.

            i-PS can also be synthesized using the Metallocene-catalysis. But can also be synthesized via Anionic-Polymerization.

        \subsection{Question 3}
        
            Commonly the degree of crystallization decides how stiff a polymer is. The higher the degree of crystallization, the harder and stiffer the polymer. 
            It also gets less ductile and more dense because crystalline parts can be packed better than amorph parts.

        \subsection{Question 5}

            To get an semi-crystalline polymer out of an polymerglass it has to be reheated. By reheating the polymer the molecular mobility gets high enough to form crystals. 
            This process is also known as cold crystallization.
